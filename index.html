Kerala, INDIA

Kerala, a state on India's tropical Malabar Coast, has nearly 600km of Arabian Sea shoreline. It's known for its palm-lined beaches and backwaters, a network of canals.
Inland are the Western Ghats, mountains whose slopes support tea, coffee and spice plantations as well as wildlife.
National parks like Eravikulam and Periyar, plus Wayanad and other sanctuaries, are home to elephants, langur monkeys and tigers.

Agra, INDIA

Agra is a city on the banks of the Yamuna river in the Agra district of the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh.
It is 206 kilometres south of the national capital New Delhi. Agra is the fourth-most populous city in Uttar Pradesh and 24th in India.
Agra is a major tourist destination because of its many Mughal-era buildings, most notably the Taj Mahal, Agra Fort and Fatehpur Sikri, all of which are UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Delhi, INDIA

The India Gate is located at the heart of India’s capital city, New Delhi. About 2.3 km from the Rashtrapati Bhavan, it is located on the eastern extremity of the ceremonial boulevard, Rajpath.
India Gate is a war memorial dedicated to honor the soldiers of the Undivided Indian Army who died during World War I between 1914 and 1921.
War memorials are buildings, installations, statues or other edifices dedicated either to celebrate victory in war, or to pay tribute to those who died or were injured in war.
Delhiites and tourists alike throng the India Gate Lawns surrounding the monument for a leisurely evening, enjoying the light show at the fountains along with snacking on street food.
A National War Memorial to honor all armed forces members killed after 1947 is under construction at the ‘C’ Hexagon of India Gate.

Ajantha and Ellora, INDIA

Ajanta is 100 kilometres (62 miles) from the Ellora Caves, which contain Hindu, Jain and Buddhist caves,the last dating from a period similar to Ajanta.
The Ajanta style is also found in the Ellora Caves and other sites such as the Elephanta Caves, Aurangabad Caves, Shivleni Caves and the cave temples of Karnataka.

Varanasi, INDIA

Varanasi grew as an important industrial centre famous for its muslin and silk fabrics, perfumes, ivory works, and sculpture.
Buddha is believed to have founded Buddhism here around 528 BCE when he gave his first sermon, "The Setting in Motion of the Wheel of Dharma", at nearby Sarnath.

Mysore Palace, INDIA

The Mysore Palace is a historical palace and the royal residence at Mysore in the Indian State of Karnataka.
It is the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and the seat of the Kingdom of Mysore.
The palace is in the centre of Mysore, and faces the Chamundi Hills eastward.

Mount Kailash & Manasarovar, INDIA

According to Hinduism, the lake was first created in the mind of the Lord Brahma after which it manifested on Earth.
In Hinduism, Lake Manasarovar is a personification of purity, and one who drinks water from the lake will go to the abode of Shiva after death.
The Kailash Mansarovar yatra is very popular for its religious significance among Indians. Lord Shiva’s devotees visit the snow-clad peaks in the Himalayas to seek his blessings.
Not everyone is eligible for this yatra because of rugged terrain, freezing temperatures, dangerous trekking trails are part of this religious yatra.
To be eligible for this yatra one should be physically fit. Not only Hindus visit this annual pilgrimage but Jains and Buddhists also have a deep respect for this yatra.
The Kailash Manasarovar Yatra is conducted via different routes every year for a select number of people.
People are selected for this yatra through a careful medical examination to check his/her physical fitness and to check that the person can adjust with the extreme conditions or not.
The yatra involves trekking at high altitudes of up to 19,500 feet, under harsh conditions, including rugged terrain and extreme cold.
Mt Kailash is regarded as the earthly manifestation of Mt Meru by Hindus. According to Hindu mythology, Svarga exit on Mt Meru and one can clean his sins by visiting Mount Kailash.
The yatra includes visiting the Manasarovar Lake Mansarovar Lake is said to be the highest freshwater lake in the world and is located at an altitude of 4,558m.
It is believed that one can be assured of reaching Lord Brahma’s paradise by taking a bath in Mansarovar Lake and by a drink of its holy water, one can be free from the sins of a hundred lifetimes.
To cover the 52 km circuit, Hindus travel clockwise around the mountain. The circuit takes 4-5 days to complete and is a little over 100 km.
